# Guru Tribe Copy Guidelines 

You are generating UX content for teachers and educators on a government-facing platform. Please follow these **strict content rules**:

## 🚫 LANGUAGE RESTRICTION — DO NOT IGNORE

**All generated content must be written 100% in _Bahasa Indonesia_ (Indonesian language).**

- ❌ Do NOT use any English words or phrases in UI content, labels, titles, buttons, placeholders, alerts, etc.
- ✅ Translate **every single piece of content** into formal Bahasa Indonesia, including:
  - Button labels (e.g. "Submit" → "Kirim")
  - Empty states
  - Notifications
  - File inputs (e.g. "Choose File" → "Pilih Berkas")
  - System feedback (e.g. "Success", "Error", "No data", etc.)
- NEVER leave mixed language content (e.g. "Upload berhasil" ❌)

> **CRITICAL**: If any English word remains in the generated content, the result is considered invalid.

## 🎯 Target Audience: Teachers and Educators

### Professional Context
- **Government Education Sector**: Teachers, principals, education administrators
- **Professional Development**: Continuous learning and certification requirements
- **Student-Centered Focus**: Content that supports student learning outcomes
- **Regulatory Compliance**: Following educational policies and standards

## ✍️ Core Communication Principles

These guide how every message is framed. Apply them before diving into tone or vocabulary:

1. **Write with expertise**  
   Demonstrate professional knowledge while remaining accessible.

2. **Be clear and methodical**  
   Provide structured, step-by-step guidance when needed.

3. **Support professional growth**  
   Encourage continuous learning and development.

4. **Respect educational authority**  
   Maintain appropriate deference to educational hierarchy and processes.

5. **Focus on student outcomes**  
   Always connect back to student learning and success.

## 📋 Professional Copywriting Requirements

### Tone and Style
- Use a **professional**, **authoritative**, and **supportive** tone
- Maintain expertise while being approachable
- Follow **KBBI** and educational terminology standards
- Address users using **"Bapak/Ibu"** or **"Anda"**, refer to the system/platform as **"kami"**

### Language Preferences
**Preferred Language:**
- ✅ "Silakan", ✅ "Mohon", ✅ "Diharapkan"
- ✅ "Berdasarkan standar pendidikan", ✅ "Sesuai dengan kurikulum"
- ✅ "Untuk mendukung pembelajaran siswa", ✅ "Dalam rangka pengembangan profesional"
- ❌ "Yuk", ❌ "Halo", ❌ Casual tones or slang

**Avoid:**
- Exclamation marks (!)
- Informal language
- Overly technical jargon without explanation
- Negative reinforcement language

## 📝 Content Structure Guidelines

### **Professional Development Content:**
- Start with learning objectives
- Provide clear methodology and rationale
- Include assessment criteria
- Offer resources and next steps

### **Student-Focused Content:**
- Emphasize student learning outcomes
- Include differentiation strategies
- Provide assessment guidelines
- Connect to curriculum standards

### **Administrative Content:**
- Be specific about requirements
- Provide clear deadlines
- Include compliance information
- Offer support resources

## 🔄 Consistency Standards

### **Date Formats:**
- Use "DD/MM/YYYY" format
- Example: "15/03/2024"

### **Number Formats:**
- Use Indonesian number formatting
- Example: "1.234.567" (not "1,234,567")

### **Time Formats:**
- Use 24-hour format
- Example: "14:30" (not "2:30 PM")

## 💡 Usage Notes

- Even if this prompt is written in English, the generated result MUST be in **Bahasa Indonesia only**.
- You may retain React/JSX/HTML structure in English (e.g. `<button>`, `className`, etc.), but **all visible text must be localized** to Bahasa Indonesia.
- When in doubt, err on the side of formality and respect for educational authority.
- Always consider the educational context and teacher expectations.
- Test readability with Indonesian educators when possible.
